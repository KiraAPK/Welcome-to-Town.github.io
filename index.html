Welcome to Town Guide

Welcome to Town is a social deduction or murder mystery game server, heavily inspired by Town of Salem, in which players are randomly and privately assigned a faction and role. Each of these factions has its specific win conditions that players must work together to achieve, typically the elimination of another faction. But who can you trust? Because roles are secret, players often lie and fake claim roles to prevent themselves from being executed. It is up to you to deduce who is friend and who is foe.

Day/Night Cycle

The game has a day/night cycle in which the game progresses. The cycle goes as following:

The Morning

  • The Morning is when the day starts. Deaths are announced at this time. The Morning phase ends when all deaths have been announced.

    • Discussion Phase

  • The Discussion Phase is a 24-hour period where living players may discuss who among them should be lynched. It is at this time that it is advised to share any information. Be careful what you believe, however.
    This phase may be skipped if enough players request.

    • Voting Phase

  • The Voting phase is when living players may choose to lynch a player. The phase ends when a player is lynched or when 12 hours have passed. There may be up to three trials in one day, but only one player may be lynched.

    • Trial

  • When a player is on trial, they are given a moment to state their defense. After which, other plays may vote Innocent or Guilty. If a player does not vote, they will automatically Abstain and their vote will not be counted.
    Once a player is found guilty, they may state their last words before they are executed, and their role and last will is revealed.

    • Night

  • Night is where most of the action happens. Players may choose to preform their actions. The night ends once everyone has taken their action. This is the time to be afraid, as you might not survive.

    • - Game-related information MUST be kept in-game. Do NOT use private messages to talk about the game.
    - Images may be attatched to last wills, but may not be posted in Day Chat. Screenshots are allowed, but only of notifications given by Game Masters.
    - Please do not intentionally throw the game. Doing so will result in instant punishment.
    - To add on to above rule: Foul play (Example: Outing mafia/coven as recruited mafia/coven) will also result in instant punishment.
    - Please do not abuse any unintended features. (For Example: Using channels all players may speak in to give information to the living as a dead player.)
    - Please do not abuse loopholes in the rules and game mechanics.
    - 3 full days of inactivity will result in your character being terminated, and a warning. Continuous inactivity will result greater punishment.

  • Once the trap is built, they may use a night action to place it on a playerâ€™s house. They may not place it on their own house naturally. The trap will remain there until it is triggered, even if the Trapper dies.

If the trap is in an unknown location, the Trapper will still know what triggered the trap, unless the Trapper is controlled that night.

    Godfather
    - This role may send a private message to another mafia member during the day. There will be no public notification, and nobody else will be able to read the message. The Godfather may only do this 2 times a day.
    - The Godfather has no night ability. However, when choosing to kill, the Godfather takes priority over the other mafia.
    - If controlled, the Godfather will be forced to kill.
    - The Godfather has basic defense.
    - The Godfather appears to be Innocent to the Sheriff, but if the Godfather kills that night, they will appear Suspicious.
    - There can only be one Godfather.
    Agent
    - This role may stalk a player at night, learning who the player visits and who visits that player.
    - Agent cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    Ambusher
    - This role has the ability to prepare an ambush outside of a player's house, attacking one visitor.
    - All visitors will learn who the Ambusher is.
    - Ambusher can target fellow Mafia members. It cannot, however, kill fellow Mafia members.
    - There can only be one Ambusher.
    Blackmailer
    - This role can blackmail a player. The blackmailed player will not be able to speak the following day.
    - The Blackmailer can read private whispers.
    - Blackmailer cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    - There can only be one Blackmailer.
    Consigliere
    - This role can investigate a player each night, learning their exact role.
    - The Arsonist and Hex Master can affect these results.
    - Consigliere cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    Disguiser
    - This role can choose to either disguise a fellow mafia member as an innocent role of their choosing, or frame an innocent player as a mafia role of their choosing.
    - The Sheriff will see the framed target as suspicious, or the disguised target as innocent.
    - The Investigator will receive the results of the role the Disguiser chooses.
    - The Psychic will see framed targets as evil, and disguised targets as innocent.
    - Disguises only last for one night.
    - Frames last until the player is checked.
    Demolitionist
    - Twice per game, this role may visit another non-Mafia player at night to pass them a bomb. They may detonate the bomb beginning the night after they pass it.
    - When a player is passed a bomb, they will be notified that "Someone passed you a bomb! Pick another player to pass it to before day/night ends." If the player was passed the bomb at night, they may chose another living player to pass the bomb to before day ends, that player being given the bomb at the end of Day. If the player was passed the bomb at night, they will pass the bomb to whoever they visit. If a player is unable to visit normally, they gain the ability to visit exclusively to pass the bomb. A player does not have to pass the bomb. The Demolitionist is notified who has the bomb whenever it is passed.
    - A player with the bomb is unable to pass it to the Demolitionist, a player being protected by GA/Adm, a jailed/kidnapped player, or the player who passed the bomb to them. They will be informed that they were unable to pass the bomb to that player (except for a jailed/kidnapped player, in which case they will be informed that their target was jailed).
    - If the player currently with the bomb dies, the bomb is defused (at night, this is the player that receives the bomb at the end of Night, not the player who received it at the end of Day). If the player with the bomb does not pass it and is protected by GA/Adm that night, the bomb is defused. If the Demolitionist dies, the bomb is defused.
    - If the Demolitionist decides to detonate the bomb, the current player with the bomb will be informed that "The bomb has started it's countdown..." and will be dealt a basic attack at the end of day/night. It will be publicly announced that "The bomb went off!"
    - There may only be one active bomb at a time. There may only be one Demolitionist.
    Forger
    - This role can forge a player's documents. If the player dies that night, their shown role/last will is replaced with what the Forger selected. The Forger can do this 2 times.
    - Forger cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    Hypnotist
    - This role can plant a memory on another player, giving them a false notification of the Hypnotist's choosing.
    - Hypnotist cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    Janitor
    - This role can clean a player's house at night. If the player dies, their role and last will is hidden. The Janitor can only do this 3 times.
    - The Janitor will be informed of the player's role and last will.
    - Janitor cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    Kidnapper
    - This role may choose one person during the day to kidnap the following night. The kidnapped players will not be able to preform any night actions. The Kidnapper may only do this three times.
    - The Kidnapper may talk anonymously with the kidnapped player, and will appear to be a Jailor. The player will be unable to talk in any team private chats.
    - The Kidnapper may choose to exterminate the player once per game. The death will appear to have been from a Jailor.
    - The Kidnapper is still able to talk in Mafia chat, but the kidnapped player can hear them.
    - The Kidnapper cannot jail the Jailor, or any player elected as Mayor, no matter their role. However, the Kidnapper can be jailed.
    - The Kidnapper cannot kidnap fellow mafia members.
    - There can only be one Kidnapper.
    Moonshiner
    - This role can offer a drink to a player at night. The player will be too drunk to perform any actions and be forced to remain home. The player will not get hungover from a Moonshiner roleblock.
    - Once per game, the Moonshiner may add poison to a drink. Poison is a direct basic attack that takes a day to kill.
    - If the Moonshiner roleblocks a Serial Killer, they will die.
    - The Moonshiner will not know if their target could not be roleblocked.
    - Moonshiner cannot target fellow Mafia members naturally.
    - The Moonshiner cannot be roleblocked.
    - There can only be one Moonshiner.

    If a Mafia role spawns alone, they will be able to kill and use their ability at the same time. This does not apply to Godfather or Kidnapper.

    Win condition varies.

    Amnesiac
    - This role has the ability to become another role from the graveyard. The Amnesiac can only become the role of players that have died.
    - The Amnesiac will not become the role until day starts. It is publicly announced when Amnesiac remembers a role.
    - If the Amnesiac is controlled into a player that dies that night, the Amnesiac will remember the role of that player.
    - Amnesiac cannot remember the role of players that have been cleaned or stoned.
    - If a unique role is alive, Amnesiac cannot remember that role, even if one exists in the graveyard. If two Amnesiacs attempt to remember the same unique role, the one who gets the role will be randomly chosen.
    - If the Amnesiac does not remember a role by the time the game ends, they lose.
    Guardian Angel
    - This role is automatically assigned a target that they must keep alive for the entire game. The Guardian Angel can protect their target twice per game, granting their target powerful defense, preventing them from being executed during the day, and purging them of any dangerous effects (e.g., being doused).
    - The Guardian Angel cannot have a Jester, Executioner or Guardian Angel as their target.
    - If the Guardian Angel's target dies, the Guardian Angel becomes an Amnesiac.
    - If the Guardian Angel's target lives to the end, the Guardian Angel wins.
    - There can only be one Guardian Angel.
    Admirer
    - Twice per game, after Night 1, this role may choose to propose to a player during the day. They will propose the following night. If the player accepts, the Admirer will become bound to them, learning their role, and can only win with them. If the Admirer's lover dies, the Admirer also dies. The Admirer can win while dead, as long as their lover is alive.
    - If two Admirers attempt to propose to the same person, only one proposal will go through. The Admirer cannot propose to someone who another admirer already proposed to.
    - The Admirer can protect their target twice per game, granting them powerful defense and purging them of any dangerous effects. Admirer cannot do this the night they propose.
    - Once per game, the Admirer may divorce their target. This can be done at any time, and their target will be informed. This cannot be done if their target is already dead. The Admirer will be silenced for a day afterward.

    Jester
    - This role, upon being publicly executed, can choose one player to deal an unstoppable attack to. The Jester can only target a player who voted guilty or abstained.
    - If the Jester is not hung or is killed in a different way, the Jester loses.
    Executioner
    - This role is randomly assigned a target at the beginning of the game. This player can be any town role, excluding Jailor. The Executioner's goal is to get their target publicly executed.
    - If the Executioner's target dies before being lynched, the Executioner becomes a Jester.
    - The Executioner has basic defense.
    - If the Executioner's target lives, the Executioner loses.

    The remaining NE all have the same win condition: Live to see the town lose the game. If there are no kill-capable roles left AND the NE is the last evil role, then they will automatically lose. Solo Coven roles will function in the same way.

    Changeling
    - This role must choose a target night 1. If they do not, one will be chosen at random. This player cannot be a Vampire, Cursed Soul, Neutral Benign, Jester or Executioner.
    - The target will be dealt an unstoppable attack. The Changeling then gains the stats, abilities and investigative results of this player, only bypassed by roles that cannot be fooled.
    - Until the Changeling replaces someone, they will have basic defense.
    - The Changeling can only replace a player once.
    Auditor
    -This role has two main abilities: Audit and Smokebomb. The Auditor may choose which ability to use.
    - Auditing a player roleblocks them and removes 1 ability charge. The Auditor will learn the victim's role, regardless of whether or not it was successful.
    - Using a smokebomb on a player obscures all feedback, preventing visitors from getting results. The Auditor will learn the victim's role.
    - The Auditor may choose to smokebomb the jailor if jailed.
    - The Auditor has a Basic Defense barrier, and will lose this barrier once attacked.
    Starspawn
    - This role may choose to isolate a player each night. All visitors will be Starbound. Starbound does not affect direct attackers. The Starspawn will know the roles of everyone who visited the target.
    - Being starbound roleblocks the victim. This will have a different message than a normal roleblock. Players immune to Starbound will know they were attempted Starbound.
    - During the day, the Starspawn may choose to activate Daybreak, which will prevent all other players from using day abilities.
    - The Starspawn has a Basic Defense barrier, and will lose this barrier once attacked.
    - There can only be one Starspawn.

    Serial Killer
    - This role can kill one player each night. Every time the Serial Killer successfully kills a player, they gain Bloodlust. After 2 stacks of Bloodlust, the Serial killer's next attack will be powerful, and the Serial Killer will also attack all players who visit their target. The Serial Killer keeps these boosts until they fail to kill someone.
    - If the Serial Killer fails to kill, their Bloodlust is reset. The failed kill is counted before any others.
    - The Serial Killer has basic defense.
    - If the Serial Killer attacks another Serial Killer, both players will know each other's roles and be able to talk to each other at night.
    - The Serial Killer can win with other Serial Killers.
    - The Serial Killer will automatically kill anyone that roleblocks them. However, the Serial Killer may choose to be cautious and not kill roleblockers, but will as a result lose their roleblock immunity .
    Shroud
    - This role has two main abilities: They may choose to deal a basic attack to a player, or may choose to shroud a player, dealing a basic attack to whoever the player visits. Shrouding a player is an Astral visit, and will not be seen by lookouts or trackers.
    - Shrouded players will be seen as an attacker to Bodyguards and Trappers.
    - If a shrouded player does not visit, the Shroud will attack the shrouded player instead.
    - The Shroud cannot spawn alone. However, a maximum of 3 shrouds may exist at once.
    - Shrouds know eachother and may win together, but may not communicate with eachother at night.
    - The Shroud has basic defense.
    Arsonist
    - This role can douse a player each night. Anyone who visits the Arsonist will automatically be doused. The Arsonist will know who is doused and if they themselves are doused. The Arsonist will not know if a player another arsonist doused is doused.
    - If the Arsonist takes no action, they will clean any gasoline off themselves.
    - The Arsonist may choose to ignite all doused targets instead, dealing an unstoppable attack to all doused targets.
    - The Arsonist has basic defense.
    - The Arsonist can win with other Arsonists.
    Werewolf
    - This role can choose to kill a player on "Full Moon nights" (even-numbered nights, or Night 5 onward), dealing a powerful attack to the target and all visitors of the target.
    - If the Werewolf takes no action, they will rampage at home instead, killing all visitors.
    - If Roleblocked, the Werewolf will either rampage at home or at the roleblocker's home, depending on what the roleblocker is.
    - The Werewolf has basic defense.
    - There can only be one Werewolf.

    Pirate
    - This role may choose a target during the day, that they will duel the following night. At the start of the night, the Pirate may choose to use their Scimitar, Rapier or Pistol, in which the victim may choose to Sidestep, use Chainmail, or Backpedal.
    - The Pirate may not target the same player twice in a row.
    - Scimitar beats Sidestep, Rapier beats Chainmail, and Pistol beats Backpedal.
    - If the target loses, the target is dealt a powerful attack. Otherwise, nothing happens.
    - Either way, the target is unable to perform any actions that night. Pirate bypasses most roleblock immunities. (Exceptions: Veteran, Retributionist, Necromancer)
    - The Pirate may anonymously talk to their victim at night.
    - If the Pirate wins two duels, the Pirate wins. The Pirate can win with anyone, even if the Pirate is dead.
    - The Pirate cannot be roleblocked or controlled.
    - There may only be one Pirate.
    Plaguebearer
    - This role may choose to infect a player with the Plague each night. Players will not know they have been infected, and can spread the Plague amongst themselves. When all living players are infected, the Plaguebearer becomes Pestilence, Horseman of the Apocalypse, gaining a powerful rampage attack and invincible defense.
    - The Plaguebearer can see which players infected who at night.
    - The Plaguebearer has basic defense.
    - There may only be one Plaguebearer.
    Insane (True All Any Exclusive)
    - The player will not know they are insane. They will believe they are a random Town role (except Jailor.) They will be able to "perform" night actions, but in reality, have no night ability and will not visit. They will be given random information if the role they believe themselves to be would gain information.
    - They will appear to be the Insane to all Investigative roles.
    - This role wins with Town, unless they become Delusional.
    - If no town members visit the Insane for 3 nights, the Insane will become Delusional.
    - The Delusional will still not know they are insane, and the player they attempt to use an ability on will be killed instead. The target is random if the Delusional believes themself to be a non-visiting role.
    - The Delusional wins if they live to see the town lose the game.
    - The Delusional has basic defense for one hit. The Delusional will not know they were attacked.
    Jackal (Only in Jackal Mode)
    - This role starts the game with two recruits with opposing win conditions. Jester, Executioner, Pirate, Vampire and Neutral Benign roles cannot be recruited. The recruits are bound to each other and can only win together. A Recruited Mafia/Coven member gains the ability to target and/or attack their original teammates.
    - If one recruit dies, the other recruit also dies.
    - If the Jackal's recruits die, the Jackal may assassinate one person each night, dealing a Powerful Attack. A player who steals the jackal's role and/or ability will act as if the recruits are dead.
    - The Jackal wins with their recruits.
    - There may only be one Jackal.
    Cursed Soul (only in Wandering Souls Mode)
    - This role may swap roles with another player. Both players will keep their original faction and win condition.
    - The Cursed Soul cannot swap with Neutral Benign, Evil or Chaos roles, except Plaguebearer or Jackal.
    - The Cursed Soul wins with other cursed souls, and will know who other Cursed Souls are. Cursed souls win if they are the last remaining faction.
    - There may be up to four Cursed Souls naturally.
    Vampire (only in Vampire Town Mode)
    - This role will be capable of converting other players into vampires (with a 1-night cooldown after a successful convert) up to a maximum of 4 vampires.
    - Any non-town aligned player without defense will be dealt a basic attack instead of being converted. The only exceptions to this rule are Amnesiac and Jester.
    - Vampires will vote on who to convert when they have the option to. Once a target is chosen, the youngest Vampire will visit that target.
    - If the target is innocent and does not have defense, the target will be converted.
    - Vampire is seen as an attacker, no matter who the target is.

    Another evil faction, rival to the Mafia. These roles, similar to the Mafia, must kill anyone who would oppose the Coven to win.

    Coven Leader
    - This role may choose to attack a player each night. A successful kill will reduce the time it takes to achieve the Necronomicon by one day.
    - With the Necronomicon, the Coven Leader gains basic defense. The Coven Leader may choose to deal a powerful attack instead, letting their guard down and making themself vulnerable to attacks while also gaining roleblock immunity.
    - Coven Leader cannot spawn alone.
    Hex Master
    - This role may hex a player each night. Hexed players will show suspicious to sheriffs and will appear to be Hex Master to Consigliere and Investigator.
    - Once every non-coven player is hexed, all hexed players are dealt with an unstoppable attack.
    - With the Necronomicon, the Hex Master's visits become Astral, and the Hex Master may also choose a second target. The second target will be hexed and dealt a basic attack.
    - Hex Master cannot spawn alone.
    Necromancer
    - This role can temporarily resurrect the corpse of a player and command the minion to use their ability on a target of the Necromancer's choice. The Necromancer cannot use the same corpse twice.
    - The Necromancer cannot be roleblocked or controlled.
    - With the Necronomicon, the Necromancer may choose to summon a ghoul to deal a basic attack to a target.
    - When alone, the Necromancer may choose to summon a ghoul to investigate a player at night.
    Banshee
    - This role may Deafen a player each night. The deafened player will be unable to read â round-2-day-chat and will be unable to receive whispers. If the Banshee attempts to deafen the same player twice, the target will be dealt a basic attack.
    - With the Necronomicon, the target will always be dealt a basic attack.
    - The Banshee can read private whispers.
    - When alone, the Banshee loses the ability to kill, but will know the role of the player they deafen.
    Poisoner
    - This role may poison one player each night. The poisoned player will be dealt a basic attack the following night unless healed by a Doctor.
    - With the Necronomicon, poison kills instantly. If the Poisoner attacks a player with natural basic defense, the player will lose their defense.
    - When alone, if the Poisoner attacks a player with natural basic defense, the player will lose their defense.
    Potion Master
    - This role has 3 potions at their disposal, each with a 2-day cooldown.
    - The Heal potion grants a coven member basic defense. Potion Master cannot target themself.
    - The Reveal potion checks a non-coven member. These results can be affected by Disguiser and Arsonist.
    - The Kill potion deals a basic attack.
    - With the Necronomicon, potions no longer have a cooldown.
    - When alone, potions have a 1-day cooldown.
    Medusa
    - This role may choose to gaze visitors 3 times per game, turning all players who visit the Medusa into stone. A player who is turned to stone will not have their role or will revealed.
    - With the Necronomicon, the Medusa gains basic defense and may directly attack a player each night.
    - When alone, the Medusa may directly attack a player each night. The Medusa will know the role of the player they attack.
    Witch
    - This role can control a player each night, forcing their victim to use their ability on a player of the Witch's choosing. The Witch will learn the exact role of their target and receive any messages their target would have received instead.
    - With the Necronomicon, the Witch gains basic attack.
    - The Witch cannot be roleblocked.
    - When alone, the Witch gains basic defense for one hit.

    All COVEN ROLES ARE UNIQUE - THERE CAN ONLY BE ONE OF EACH
    If a coven role spawns alone, it will be a Neutral Evil instead. Their necronomicon ability will be replaced with a immediate (but weaker) ability, and they will show Innocent to the Sheriff.

    The Mafia

  • Mafia members may volunteer to kill. Once the player is chosen, that player will choose who to kill instead of using their normal ability.
  • If more than one player volunteers, the player that kills will be randomly chosen. Only one player may kill at a time
  • If nobody volunteers, there will be no kill.

    • The Coven

  • On Night 3, one member of the Coven will receive the Necronomicon. The Necronomicon will boost the abilities of the player who holds it.
  • Necronomicon Priority: Coven Leader, Hex Master, Potion Master, Necromancer, Banshee, Poisoner, Witch -(THESE 5 ARE RANDOM), Medusa

  • Attack VS. Defense

  • None: Cannot kill/Has no defense.
  • Basic: Basic attack is capable of killing any player with no defense.
  • Powerful: Powerful attack is capable of killing any player under powerful defense.
  • Unstoppable: Unstoppable attack is capable of killing any player under invincible defense (Only one role currently exists capable of invincible defense.)

    • Barriers

  • Some roles have temporary defense. These include: Witch, Changeling and Jackal.
  • These roles will have basic defense until they are attacked. If they are attacked again afterwards, they will die.
  • An evil role attacking a role with a barrier will display a unique Defense message. The same message will display for protections preformed by non-town roles.

    • Roleblocks and Hangover

  • When a Tavern Keeper roleblocks a player, they become Hungover. This means they cannot be roleblocked again for one night. Moonshiner ignores this.
  • Some roles cannot be roleblocked. These roles include:
  • Tavern Keeper
  • Retributionist
  • Veteran
  • Moonshiner
  • Serial Killer (unless cautious)
  • Pirate
  • Coven Leader (if empowered attack)
  • Necromancer
  • Witch

    • Last Wills and Death Notes

  • Last wills are shown to everyone when a player dies. At any time, a player may choose to edit their last will. Be careful what you put in it. You could either save or screw over your team.
  • Death notes are written by the attacker (most evil roles can do this) and are shown to everyone when the attacker kills a player. At any time, a killer may choose to edit their death note. Be careful what you put in it. Some information benefits the town more than you.

    • Voting, Trials, and the Election

  • During the day, the town may discuss information they gathered during the night. Be wary of those who may lie and feed false information.
  • Using the information discussed, players may vote on who they think is most suspicious.
  • Voting onto the Stand: Players may vote on who they think is most suspicious. The amount of votes needed to send someone onto the stand will vary depending on the town's population (will require a majority).
  • Trials: There are 3 trials per day cycle. If a player is executed then the day ends, regardless of remaining trials.
  • Verdicts: Vote either Innocent or Guilty. You may choose to not vote and instead Abstain.
  • On Day 3, and Day 3 only, players may vote to elect a mayor during the discussion phase. Electing a Mayor will function in the same way as a normal trial.

    • Whispering

  • You may privately whisper to a player. To do this, in your private channel, type "Whisper (player): '(message)'." It will be sent to them, but everyone will know you whispered to them, so make sure to prepare to be questioned!
  • Blackmailers and Banshees can read everyone's whispers.

    • Blackmailed

  • When a player is blackmailed, they cannot talk or whisper, though they still can be whispered to, and they can still vote.
  • If put on trial, the blackmailed player may say "I am blackmailed." and nothing else.

    • Deafened

  • When a player is deafened, they cannot see other players' messages or whispers, though they can still speak while on trial, and they can still vote.
  • If deafened again the following night, unless the player is protected or has their own defense, they will die. This is only if the Banshee is not alone.
